Blue Whale Population in the Pacific Ocean

Current Population Estimate:
- Approximately 3,000-5,000 blue whales in the North Pacific Ocean

Key Points:
- Blue whale populations have dramatically decreased from hundreds of thousands to just a few thousand
- The population has been reduced by approximately 99% due to historical whaling
- Ongoing conservation efforts aim to protect and monitor blue whale habitats

Conservation Status:
- Considered endangered species
- Populations are slowly recovering but remain critically low

Sources:
1. Recent marine mammal population surveys
2. Conservation research data from marine biology organizations
